Edsgar Wybe Dijkstra

Edsger Wybe Dijkstra was a Dutch systems scientist, programmer, software engineer, science essayist, and early pioneer in computing science.

Timeline

  • 1930 - Born in Rotterdam, Netherlands
  • 1952 - Became the Netherlands' first "programmer" as he started to work at the Mathematisch Centrum in Amsterdam
  • 1956 - Formulated and solved the shortest path problem for a demonstration at the official inauguration of the ARMAC computer
  • 1957 - Married Maria (Ria) C. Debets
  • 1968 - Wrote a letter to the editor entitled "GO TO Statement Considered Harmful"
  • 1972 - Won Turing Award
  • 1984 - Started as the Schlumberger Centennial Chair in Computer Sciences at the University of Texas at Austin
  • 1999 - Retired from the Schlumberger Centennial Chair in Computer Sciences at the University of Texas at Austin
  • 2002 - Died in Nuenen, Netherlands
